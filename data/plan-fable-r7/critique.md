# Cross-review critique: plan-sol-w2 draft vs plan-fable-r7 draft

**Reviewer:** plan-fable-r7 author. Inputs: `/Users/leebarry/firstmate/data/plan-sol-w2/report.md` (read in full), `/Users/leebarry/firstmate/data/kzero-review-findings.md`, my own draft, and the code reading behind it.

**Verdict in one paragraph:** the two drafts agree on every foundational bet — no ECS, one fixed-tick driver in `useBeforePhysicsStep`, integer ticks + seeded RNG + local-replay determinism (not lockstep), dynamic-body hover craft kept, server-authoritative race/combat *rules* with client-authoritative movement as an explicit casual-tier compromise, energy-as-boost-and-health, exact 60-tick in-place respawn, bot-driven QA with the same intent interface as players, CC0-first assets, no motion blur. That convergence should be treated as settled. The real synthesis work is in ~10 divergences, of which I concede three outright to sol-w2 (RMF frames now, drag-based speed envelope, tighter perf budgets with payload gates), defend three of mine (a lateral dodge verb, the absorb mechanic, simpler netcode without the reconciliation tiers), and propose merges for the rest. Both drafts missed the same two live bugs named in the findings doc (dead `reportCheckpointCrossed`, lobby reset dead-end) and both under-specified the interim local-vs-server race lifecycle, which the findings doc explicitly asks us to decide.

## 1. Strongest ideas in sol-w2 to adopt

1. **Track compiler promoted before the vehicle controller, with parallel-transport (RMF) frames from day one** (§2.1, P1.2). Their argument is better than my "world-up now, RMF in Phase 6": the controller v2, AI line, reset frames, and spatial index all consume compiled frames, so tuning feel *before* the frame representation stabilizes risks a retune; and since both plans rewrite `buildTrack` anyway, RMF costs ~1–2 extra days behind golden fixtures. My draft's own Phase-6 loop ambition then stops being a physics-and-geometry double jump. **I update my position: adopt.** (Caveat below in §2.1 about not letting P1 get slow.)
2. **`CompiledTrack` as a validated product** — seam/orthogonality/width/clearance validation, `track:validate` CLI, SVG top-down preview, sub-10-second definition-to-play, AI/reset/minimap data in the same compile output, justified by the WipEout Pure postmortem (spline pipeline as the production bottleneck-breaker). This is the best single section of their draft. Adopt wholesale, including 40–80 m render/collider chunks (free frustum culling); defer their streaming machinery — v1 tracks are small enough to load whole, and per-track *manifests* (also theirs) solve the real 86 MB problem.
3. **Frequency/damping-ratio suspension parameterization** (`k = (2πf)²·m_pad`, `c = 2ζ√(km)`, explicit 900 kg mass, per-pad force clamp in g). More principled than my "keep k=320/c=30 and re-verify": mass changes stop invalidating the tune. Adopt, with one guard: first *derive* the current tuned craft's implied f/ζ and reproduce today's feel as the A/B baseline before changing values.
4. **Handling acceptance metrics table** (§3.7: ride-height RMS < 0.08 m at speed, mirrored-steering symmetry within 2%, wall-glance ≥ 80% tangent retention, camera apex-visibility ≥ 1.25 s). My draft gated feel on "subjective sign-off"; theirs makes feel regressions machine-checkable. Adopt as-is.
5. **Fatal boost** (§3.5): boosting can drain you to zero and destroy you, with a "fatal boost" UI preview below threshold rather than a silent floor. More F-Zero-authentic and more dramatic than my energy-floor-5 engage gate; the HUD preview answers the fairness objection. Adopt.
6. **Control-loss caps and anti-dominance clamps** (§4.2): stuns ≤ 450 ms, slows ≤ 25%/1.2 s with diminishing returns, no single non-combo one-shot from full energy, per-family 120 ms re-hit immunity. My draft had balance *rationale*; theirs has balance *guardrails*. Adopt verbatim.
7. **Balance gates over 1,000 seeded bot races** (§4.5) — item damage-share ≤ 22%, seeker hit-rate bands with/without countermeasures, pickups-off P1-win-rate delta < 8 pp, "skilled no-item Elite beats item-using Rookie > 70%". Strictly better than my kill-share bands; adopt and merge mine in.
8. **Competition-director perceptual rules** (§5.5): banding disabled in time trial/Elite/ranked, no adjustment in the final 20% of the last lap, none while an AI is visibly within 25 m, pace factor hard-capped 0.97–1.03, behavior-first knobs. The "not on camera" rules are the part my draft lacked and are exactly what makes rubber-banding invisible. Adopt, merged with my GX-style continuous gap-throttle + dead-zone as the underlying mechanism.
9. **Remote craft as soft/non-physical proxies in online v1, with server-validated ram damage** (§6.5). My draft specified craft-craft collision physics offline and simply never resolved what happens online — two clients resolving mutual impulses on interpolated ghosts and feeding divergent positions back is a real oscillation risk. This is a genuine gap in my draft that theirs closes. Adopt.
10. **`discontinuityId` on transforms + clock-sync from reducer timestamps** (§6.4): a monotonic counter that clears interpolation history on respawn/teleport is cleaner than my implicit respawn-flag handling, and server-time HUD countdowns need the clock offset anyway. Adopt both.
11. **Match-scoped schema and subscriptions** (§6.2): lobby/match/racer_state split with compound indexes, retiring the `SELECT * FROM …` global subscriptions (`src/net/spacetime.ts:53`). My draft kept single-room scope for v1 and deferred rooms; theirs is right that the *schema* should be match-keyed from the first migration even if the UI only offers one lobby — retrofitting match scoping after combat tables exist is much worse. Adopt.
12. **Gated `__KZERO_TEST__` contract with mutation hooks compiled out of production** (§10.1), disposable-database multiplayer integration lab, network-fault proxy matrix, and schema-migration tests (§10.6). All adopt. One reconciliation needed: bot playtests then require a `test` build flavor (Vite mode with hooks compiled in) distinct from the release build; the release checklist verifies hooks are absent.
13. **Operational evidence discipline**: they ran `pnpm build` (surfacing the 2.26 MB minified Rapier chunk and >500 KB chunk warnings), attempted the module build (surfacing that the `spacetime` CLI is absent — now a P4.1 bootstrap task), and ran the generator credential probe (**all three keys MISSING** — so every "generate it" route in *both* drafts is blocked until keys are configured, which must appear on the roadmap, not be discovered in Phase 5). My draft has none of this; adopt their findings and their habit.
14. **Layered environment composition contract** (§7.2: play/near/mid/far/motion layers with distance bands, 60–120 m readability corridor, landmark every 8–12 s, "no billboard occludes the next 2 s of track") and the **left-cyan/right-amber asymmetric edge coding** (§2.3) — an orientation channel my uniform cyan edges lack. Adopt both. Also their accessibility set (colorblind symbol mode, flash reduction, reduced-motion) which my draft under-specified.

## 2. Concrete disagreements, with rationale

**2.1 Phase-1 shape: their ordering is right, their pacing is too slow.** sol-w2's P1 is five PRs (M, L, L, M, L) before the first "fun" milestone; the brief's Phase-1 instruction is to reach a *fun time-trial fast*. My draft got a feel package landed second. Resolution: keep their order (runtime → compiler → controller → camera/slice) but insert a **small "quick feel wins" PR right after the runtime PR**, containing only frame-representation-independent items: camera package (FOV curve + punch, trauma shake, height/distance curves, lookahead), speed-line shader, display-speed multiplier, coast rule. None of these touch frames or the controller, so nothing is retuned later, and the build gets noticeably better in week one. Also adopt their S/M/L sizing and the 800-line PR split rule.

**2.2 Movement reconciliation tiers (their §6.4): cut them.** Their soft-correction ladder (0.35 m visual damping / 1.5 m physics blend / 8 m hard reset) presumes the server can produce a *better* pose than the client. In their own architecture the server never simulates movement — the echoed state is the client's own accepted publication, so below the rejection threshold there is nothing to correct *toward*. The machinery (input batch upload, 2 s prediction ring, seq/ack codecs) is real cost with no v1 payoff, and their stage-5 note ("future competitive authority") already concedes true server movement is post-v1. Keep my simpler model — client-auth movement + server plausibility validation + hard resync on rejection (the respawn-flag path that already exists, upgraded with their `discontinuityId`) — and adopt their `submit_input_batch` only as *telemetry for offline cheat review*, not as a reconciliation input. If the synthesis wants their ladder anyway, it should first name the correction source.

**2.3 Hitscan lag compensation (their §6.5): reject the 150 ms rewind.** Rewinding interpolated transform history server-side implies storing per-racer position history in tables and trusting 15–20 Hz samples to reconstruct 150 ms — at 78 m/s a racer moves ~4–5 m *between samples*, so the rewind's precision is illusory. My track-space resolution with generous hit corridors (Δs ≤ 4 m, Δlat ≤ 2.5 m) against latest transforms achieves the same arcade-grade fairness with no history storage. Converge on: guided/area/trap weapons advanced by the server combat tick in track coordinates (my §6.4); instant weapons validated at fire time as a corridor check on latest transforms (their swept-segment idea, minus rewind). Their scheduled `resolve_projectile` reducers and my 20 Hz `combat_tick` are the same mechanism — one scheduled-table pattern, keep whichever reads cleaner in the module.

**2.4 A dodge verb is missing from their handling model.** Their §3.3 has steer/drift/counter-steer-assist but no lateral dodge, while their own §4.3 counterplay column says "hard switchback" against seekers and their combat principles demand two responses per offense. A driving-line wiggle at 78 m/s is not a dodge. My Wipeout-derived **sideshift** (double-tap lateral impulse, ~0.9 s cooldown) and per-side airbrakes are the movement half of combat counterplay and cheap to build on the existing lateral-grip model. Keep them. (Their automatic counter-steer *assist* tiers are complementary — adopt those too as accessibility, alongside my raw counter-input torque bonus for skill expression.)

**2.5 The absorb mechanic is missing from their roster.** Their Nanite Repair item partially covers sustain, but Wipeout HD's absorb (convert held item → energy, per-item values) is structurally better: it turns *every* pickup into a fire-vs-heal decision, gives trailing players agency without more catch-up items, and feeds the same energy economy as boost and kills. It costs one input (hold item key) and a table. Adopt absorb into the merged roster; their per-item damage numbers then double as absorb-value anchors.

**2.6 Recharge: GX-style full-speed strips, not their slow-lane pits.** Their energy pits recharge only "within a deliberately slower outer lane" — an explicit speed tax. The GX-proven model charges at full speed and prices the recharge through *placement* (off the optimal line, so the cost is line deviation and commitment). Forced-slow lanes feel bad in a game whose whole promise is speed; placement cost achieves the same decision. Keep my model, adopt their language that pit placement is an authored routing decision. (Their 1000-point energy scale, 120/s boost drain ≈ GX's 8.3 s full-bar, and 500-on-respawn are all fine — adopt the 1000 scale and start from their proven-rate numbers rather than my deliberately hotter 30%/s drain; my hotter variant becomes a bot-duel tuning experiment, not the default.)

**2.7 Respawn pose: split the difference, in their direction.** Mine projected the death pose onto the track cross-section immediately (never spawn over void); theirs spawns at the literal death pose and lets ordinary off-track recovery run afterward (two-stage). Theirs honors the brief's "at the location where they were killed" more literally but produces a visible double-teleport when death occurs over void. Merge: respawn at the **literal death pose when it is on/above drivable surface** (suspension rays would hit within range); otherwise project to the same-`s` center-line frame — and log the projected case in telemetry as theirs suggests. Everything else about respawn is already identical in both drafts (tick+60 exact, 500/1000 energy, 250 ms non-solid + 1 s damage grace, server-arbitrated timestamp, unit tests at +59/+60/+61).

**2.8 Track 2 concept: theirs, with one feature gated.** Black Rain Foundry (industrial sodium-amber/acid-green, rain, helix) beats my Vector Sunset on art-direction grounds — it proves the "cyberpunk ≠ purple bloom" range their §7.1 argues and my palette section under-delivers on. But its 65° mag-wall quietly depends on track-relative gravity that their §3.1 only partially commits to (0.7 g local downforce). Gate the mag-wall on a physics spike; cap bank at ~45–50° if the spike fails; keep Vector Sunset as the track-3 concept. Their sequencing of track 2 *after* AI (P3.3, so bots can gate it) is better than my Phase-5 placement — adopt.

**2.9 Ship stats: light trades, not cosmetic-only.** Their three silhouette families are "handling-equivalent … not pay-to-win." Nothing is paid here, and stat trades (Body/accel/top-speed) are a core genre fantasy (GX's machine settings, Wipeout's ship stats) and a replay lever. My draft gated stat variants on a bot-verified lap-parity band (±3%); that answers the balance concern while keeping identity mechanical, not just visual. Merge: their silhouette/livery system + my bounded stat profiles.

**2.10 Minor corrections to their draft.** (a) Internal inconsistency: the planning-ledger line says "one **120 Hz** fixed simulation" while §1.4 and every respawn spec use 60 Hz/60 ticks = 1 s — presumably a typo; synthesis should say 60. (b) Minimap top-center sits adjacent to the high-speed sightline and their own HUD rule keeps alerts out of the focal column; corner placement (my draft) is safer — verify with the readability playtest rather than by fiat. (c) JSON+Zod track authoring adds a validation layer v1 doesn't need — TS data modules are already type-checked and diffable; keep the `track:validate` CLI on the *compiled* output regardless. (d) Their §2.3 says bloom "belongs to rails/signals, never the road" — agreed, but note current boost pads are road-plane emissives; reconcile as "no *continuous* road-surface bloom; discrete authored strips allowed."
