## 1. Executive summary

P4 ships multiplayer v1 as **server-validated client movement plus server-authoritative discrete state** on SpacetimeDB Maincloud (the captain's existing subscription; no self-hosting anywhere in this plan). Each client keeps simulating its own craft locally at 60 Hz in Rapier exactly as today — preserving the P1.2/P1.4 feel contract with zero added input latency and zero own-craft network corrections by construction — and publishes poses at 15 Hz through the N0 validated relay, hardened with artifact-derived path plausibility. The server (TypeScript module, reducers only) is sole authority over everything disputable: match lifecycle, admission, laps and results, item pickups and inventory, energy outcomes, damage, kills, respawn tokens. Combat resolves per weapon class: Arc and EMP-Quake server-instant, Rail by server-scheduled resolve behind its own 0.65 s telegraph, Mines victim-triggered with a server backstop, Pulse and Seeker shooter-claimed inside server plausibility envelopes.

The x9 alternative — a pure-TS movement core running 60 Hz substeps inside 50 ms scheduled reducer transactions with client prediction/reconciliation — is **rejected for v1** and recorded in §3/§4 with its revisit trigger. The short version of the merits: on SpacetimeDB's only transport (reliable, ordered WebSocket), x9's own input policy (≤ +2-tick server buffer, neutral-fill after 6 missed ticks, strict no-rewind) converts routine TCP head-of-line bursts into corrections of the player's *own* craft — the one degradation this product cannot absorb — while its prerequisite (a feel-identical pure rewrite of suspension/slab/wall/contact physics) is a phase-sized, fail-stop research project that would queue the captain-mandated GAMEPLAY_HASH handshake behind the least predictable work item in either plan. x9's genuine advantages (provable movement integrity, symmetric contacts) are worth less than that price at this product tier; the brief itself asks for anti-cheat "proportionate to a browser game."

What the converged plan takes from x9 anyway is substantial: the `admission`-row handshake, private room codes, `protocol_version`/`balance_version`, connection-generation duplicate-tab policy, dev/stage/prod database strategy with canary, the 45 s post-winner finish window, artifact-corridor pose plausibility, durable combat-entity rows, atomicity-correct telemetry, statistical rigor in the P3 gate, and budget-style measured acceptance criteria throughout. The plan also adopts x9's honesty demand: the trust model is stated plainly (§5) and v1 results are unranked.

Hard constraint delivered: the GAMEPLAY_HASH handshake (§9) — `{compiler_version, gameplay_hash}` frozen per match, admission-row rejection of mismatched clients, and a first-class client `incompatible-version` state — lands in the **first module PR (M1)**, independent of all combat and balance work, enabled by the split schema freeze (§7).

Sequencing: one pre-P4 fix PR (§6), then the P3-exit racing-fairness gate (M0), then eight single-PR milestones (§15). Two captain decisions are called out up front (§16 Q1, Q2): the scope of the P3 balance gate given that AI item use is **not** landed (contrary to the brief), and sign-off on the split schema freeze.

## 4. The netcode disagreement, resolved on the merits

### 4.1 What x9 argued, fairly stated
Input-only authority makes movement, gates, energy, and hits *provable*: the browser submits nothing but quantized input frames (the landed 11-field `QuantizedIntent` is the right vocabulary — both drafts agree), so a modified client can play perfectly but cannot fabricate state. Contacts become symmetric. The pure combat world and tick constants port naturally into 60 Hz substeps. SpacetimeDB officially supports interval-scheduled reducers at this cadence (API shape, not an SLA — both sides agree it must be measured). x9's cross-review also correctly showed that c5's original draft over-claimed in places (§6, D4–D9 corrections) and that "server-authoritative outcomes" must not be advertised as server-*observed* combat — the converged plan adopts that language discipline (§5).

### 4.2 Why it loses for v1

**(a) The transport turns its worst case into own-craft corrections.** SpacetimeDB's single channel is reliable/ordered WebSocket (x9:130 concedes; no unreliable datagrams exist to switch to). x9's input pipeline: server accepts at most +2 ticks of future input (~33 ms buffer), holds a missing input sticky ≤ 6 ticks, then simulates **neutral**; late frames are stale and dropped; the server never rewinds (x9:130, 226). One lost TCP segment at 100 ms RTT stalls all subsequent input for ≥ 1×RTT (often 200 ms+ with RTO floors); at 1 % loss and 20 packets/s that is roughly one event per 5 s of racing. During each stall the server simulates neutral (thrust off, steer centered) while the local predictor used the player's real inputs; reconciliation then drags the player's craft to a trajectory they never drove — at 70 m/s into a corner, far beyond x9's own 3 m/12° snap threshold (x9:133). This is architectural on this transport: fixing it requires server-side input rollback (excluded by x9's design, and it would multiply the tick-transaction budget) or a bigger input-delay buffer (which re-introduces the stick latency the model exists to avoid). Under the converged model the identical burst delays ghost updates and outcome events; the player's own craft is untouched because nothing upstream is permitted to correct it.

**(b) Its headline advantage mispredicts on your own hull.** Server-resolved racer contacts use remote poses the local predictor cannot know (remotes are interpolated 100–150 ms behind — x9:133). At 70–88 m/s that is ~10 m of unknowable displacement, so pack contact — the moment that sells the feature — is exactly when the player's own craft gets snapped to the server's version of the collision. x9's draft never addresses contact misprediction; its correction budgets are specified only for clean latency.

**(c) The prerequisite is a fail-stop research project in front of the mandated handshake.** The pure movement core must reproduce 4-pad suspension on curved 45–50°-banked ribbons, slab containment at up to 105 m/s, tuned wall glances, airborne/landing, and contact impulses — feel-identically, bit-stably, in fresh code. The repo's own history (the P1 fall-through saga and its dedicated real-Rapier regression gate, `src/game/physics/fallthrough.test.ts`) says surface containment at speed is hard *with* a real physics engine. x9 prices this as one PR, gives it undefined pass thresholds ("agreed targets" that don't exist yet), grants it stop-the-line semantics with no permitted fallback (x9:374: "do not ship client-transform authority"), and sequences schema freeze — hence the handshake — behind it (x9:16). It also ships a permanent two-physics fork (solo Rapier vs online pure core; x9:131), taxing every future tuning change.

**(d) Its no-rewind combat policy regresses the most-used weapon.** Pulse spawns server-side against *current* poses while the shooter aimed at 100–150 ms-stale renders; lag compensation is granted to Arc only (x9:226-234). Visually perfect shots systematically miss, and hit confirms arrive a round trip late. The converged model resolves travel-time projectiles in the shooter's perceived frame under server envelopes, preserving the landed hit feel.

**(e) Load asymmetry.** Converged model steady state: 120 small validate-and-write transactions/s per 8-player match, zero sustained sim CPU, everything else edge-triggered. x9: 160 input tx/s + 20 heavy tick tx/s (24 racer-substeps each) + ~480 input-row inserts/s + snapshot/history writes, with a soft-real-time p95 target on shared billed infrastructure and a policy of killing live races on scheduler debt (x9:129). Neither side has maincloud measurements (M1 closes that for the converged model), but the converged model's unknowns are cost-shaped; x9's are latency-shaped.

### 4.3 What x9's cross-review of c5 changed in this plan
Accepted and folded in (details in §3, §6): admission row over error parsing; atomicity-correct telemetry; track locked at create; private room codes; mine server backstop (a hacked victim can omit `trigger_mine` — the backstop detects overlap from the victim's own published poses, which movement caps constrain); boost-inference drain (a client cannot report `boosting=false` at super-terminal speeds); corridor plausibility (path legality); durable seeker rows; honest acceptance criteria for claim validation (the original "0 % fabricated hits" gate was impossible — a fabrication constructed inside a plausibility envelope is by definition indistinguishable; the converged gate is an adversarial suite that *enumerates* which fabrications are rejected and bounds the rest by rate/damage caps); the countdown-bug reframing (§6); softened DB-name wording (§6); "15 Hz proven fine" downgraded to "15 Hz implemented and locally proven; cloud-qualified in M1"; maincloud metering described as multi-dimensional rather than "per reducer execution".

Not accepted: x9-cr's final recommendation (proceed with server authority behind spike gates, relabel c5 as fallback-after-re-scope). Reasons in §4.2 stand un-rebutted by that review — it does not address the burst/neutral-fill chain, contact misprediction, or the Pulse inversion. Its strongest legitimate demand — do not oversell the trust model — is honored in §5 instead.

### 4.4 Revisit trigger for the rejected architecture
If the captain later wants ranked/competitive play (movement integrity as a product requirement rather than a nice-to-have), fund x9's authority-core + maincloud cadence spike **as its own phase** with pre-agreed handling-parity thresholds. Because the converged plan already settles all discrete outcomes server-side, that upgrade replaces the pose channel and combat *detection* while keeping the schema's authority spine — a real but honest migration (x9-cr §"Upgrade path" correctly notes it is more than "only the pose channel"). Three mandatory modifications if revived: extend pose-history rewind to projectile sweeps, replace `server_overrun` match termination with degradation, and keep the balance program off that spike's critical path.

## 5. Trust model, stated plainly (adopted from x9's honesty demand)

v1 is **server-validated, not server-observed**: the server proves speed, displacement, path corridor, gate proximity, respawn poses, inventory provenance, damage arithmetic, counterplay windows, and results; it does not observe where a craft "really" was between 15 Hz publishes nor whether a claimed Pulse/Seeker trajectory truly occurred inside its envelope. A modified client can: drive a perfect-but-corridor-legal line, land envelope-legal fabricated Pulse/Seeker hits at its inventory-capped rate, and evade mine triggers until the 15 Hz backstop catches the overlap. It cannot: exceed the speed/teleport/corridor envelope, skip gates, fire unheld weapons, exceed damage tables or immunity/grace windows, dodge boost drain at super-terminal speeds, self-respawn, or alter results. This is proportionate to unranked friends lobbies; v1 results are explicitly unranked and the UI copy will not claim cheat-proofing. This paragraph, verbatim-ish, belongs in the P4 section of `CLAUDE.md` at M8.

## 7. Generated data and the split schema freeze (synthesis; captain sign-off = Q2)

**Generated data (M1):** `pnpm track:build` emits trackData v2 for **every** REGISTRY entry (today only Neon writes `moduleDataPath` — `cli.ts:28-41`): per track `{trackId, trackVersion, compilerVersion, gameplayHash, checkpointCount, gridPoses, pickupSockets, rechargeStrips, respawnPoses, per-gate minSplit}`. A sibling `pnpm combat:gen` emits the shared pure-rules package (D14) — weapon damages/caps, energy rates, respawn/ghost/grace timing, speed caps — from `weaponTuning.ts`/`energyTuning.ts`/`craft/tuning.ts`, with a `track:check`-style drift gate wired into `pnpm test`. Rationale is the proven drift bug (§6.1): every shared constant is generated, never hand-typed.

**Split freeze:**
- **FREEZE-1 (at M1): lifecycle + movement protocol.** Match/admission/participant/transform/pickup-availability tables, the handshake tuple, input-free relay validation rules. Contains **zero balance-sensitive fields** — pickup *contents*, damage, and energy rates are data behind `balance_version`, not schema. Precondition: **M0 passes** — so the captain's "P3 exit gate before schema freeze" ordering is satisfied under the reading that the gate's suites are the racing balance/fairness suites (both tracks × 8 bots). This is the load-bearing interpretation the captain must confirm (Q1/Q2).
- **FREEZE-2 (before M5): combat constants = `balance_version 1`.** Gated on whichever balance evidence the captain selects in Q1. Combat *schema* (combat_state/entity/event shapes) is reviewed at M1 and frozen with FREEZE-2; only additive migrations after (and the event-table flag, being publish-immutable per x9's doc citation — verify in M1 — is declared at FREEZE-1 time).

This decouples the mandated handshake from the balance program entirely — the single worst schedule coupling in x9's draft and the vaguest gate in c5's, removed simultaneously.

## 8. Schema and reducers (converged)

Base is c5 §3.2-3.3 with x9's structures merged; identity stays PK on per-racer tables (one match per identity preserves every N0 authority check). Full column detail lives in the c5 draft; deltas and headline shape:

**Public:** `server_info {protocol_version, min_client_protocol}`; `track` + child tables (grid/respawn/strips/sockets) seeded from generated data; `match {id, status, track_id, compiler_version, gameplay_hash, track_version, protocol_version, balance_version, lap_count, seed, max_players, listed, host_identity?, countdown/started/finished/results_lock timestamps, created_at}` — contract triple **frozen at creation** (D8: track locked at create); `admission {identity PK, match_id?, status: admitted|incompatible|full|closed, reason_code, expected/received tuple fields, updated_at}` (D6); `participant` (N0 shape + `match_id`, `craft_family`, `kills`, `deaths`, `reject_count`, `connection_generation`); `transform` (N0 shape + `match_id`, `boosting`); `combat_state` (energy + lazy-settlement clock, slots, shield/specter/overdrive/nanite windows, stun/slow, per-family immunity timestamps, destroyed/respawn/ghost/grace); `pickup_state {match_id, socket_index, respawn_at?, claim_seq}`; `mine_entity`, `seeker_entity` (D13), `rail_charge`; `combat_event` (`event: true`) for fire/hit/utility/kill/respawn/deny; `match_result` (immutable, survives janitor).

**Private:** `room_secret {code unique, match_id, expires_at}` (D9); scheduled tables — per-match one-shots only (start, finish-timeout at 300 s ceiling + **45 s post-winner window** [x9], DNF grace, respawn-due, rail-resolve, energy-zero) plus a long-interval janitor. **No sustained per-tick schedule exists anywhere** — that is D1.

**Reducers:** `create_match{track_id, …}` (freezes tuple, mints code, joins creator), `join_match{code|id, name, color, craft_family, compiler_version, gameplay_hash, protocol_version}` (writes `admission`; §9), `leave_match`, `set_ready`, `set_ship` (lobby-only), `publish_transform` (N0 validation + generated caps + corridor plausibility + countdown grid clamp + lazy energy settlement + boost inference + strip/nanite integration + mine backstop overlap check), `cross_checkpoint{n}` (per-track count/splits + gate-OBB proximity), `request_fall_respawn` (N0 token flow), `claim_pickup`, `absorb_item`, `fire_weapon`, `claim_hit`, `trigger_mine`, `use_utility`, `request_rematch`, `kick_participant` (host), scheduled `start_race`/`finish_timeout`/`apply_dnf`/`apply_respawn`/`resolve_rail`/`energy_zero_check`/`janitor`, and connect/disconnect hooks with N0's 15 s ghost-grace semantics per match. Every reducer gets a pure mirror in `module/src/matchRules.ts` (N0's proven CLI-free test pattern, `scripts/test.mjs:54`). Soft rejections (rate/plausibility) write `reject_count` + reason and return normally; hard throws are reserved for protocol-impossible input (D7).

**Subscriptions:** identity-first (`server_info`, `track`+children, own `admission`), then match-scoped SQL filters after admission; the current `SELECT *` on all tables (`spacetime.ts:86-91`) is removed. Budget: ~120 transform row-updates/s fan-out per subscriber at 8 racers — N0's proven order of magnitude, cloud-qualified by the M1 measurement.

## 9. GAMEPLAY_HASH handshake (hard constraint — lands at M1)

1. **One truth chain:** `pnpm track:build` emits both tracks' contracts into client artifacts (`TRACK_CATALOG[key].artifact.contract`, `catalog.ts:34-55`) *and* the module registry (§7) from the same compile; `pnpm track:check` fails on drift of either.
2. Module `init`/publish seeds `track` rows; `create_match` freezes `{compiler_version, gameplay_hash, track_version}` from `track` onto the match row. Frozen means a mid-lifecycle module republish cannot mutate a live match's contract; a rematch re-runs admission if anything changed.
3. `join_match` compares the client-supplied tuple — read from the client's own bundled artifact for the match's track — against the frozen match row. Mismatch: upsert `admission{status: "incompatible", reason_code, expected_*, received_*}`, insert **no** participant/transform rows, grant no further reducer rights (every gameplay reducer requires an admitted participant), and **return normally** so the outcome commits and replicates (D6). `protocol_version` (module API) and `balance_version` mismatches produce distinct reason codes on the same row.
4. Client pre-checks against its `track`/`match` subscription before calling (no doomed call for honest clients); the server check is the enforcement.
5. UI: `incompatible-version` is a first-class online-shell state — full-screen card naming server vs local `trackVersion/hash` (technical tuple in a disclosure), "Refresh to update" CTA, Back action. Distinct copy for protocol vs track vs balance mismatch. Not a console error.
6. Deploy runbook: module and Vercel client build from the same commit; module-first ordering minimizes the mismatch window; either order fails safely by construction. Acceptance tests: stale-client × both tracks, wrong-compiler-right-hash, right-compiler-wrong-hash, protocol mismatch, direct reducer calls without admission, reconnect across a module hot-swap, and a production-build assertion that no mismatched identity can reach countdown.

## 12. Anti-cheat (converged, proportionate)

Layers: N0 carry-overs (authority checks, finite poses, generated speed cap, elapsed-time teleport cap, one-time respawn tokens, monotonic idempotent checkpoints with per-track splits) + P4 additions: corridor plausibility (D3), gate-OBB proximity, countdown grid clamp, server-owned inventory (one fire per pickup, position-validated claims), server damage tables/immunity/grace/no-one-shot, boost inference (D5), mine backstop (D4), per-identity rate caps, soft-reject telemetry with auto-DNF threshold and host kick (D7, D16), artifact-derived lap-time floor flagging results as `suspect` rather than blocking. Explicitly out of scope (documented): aim/input-pattern detection, client attestation, replay adjudication, global reputation. Trust model per §5; v1 unranked.

## 13. Testing strategy (converged)

1. **Pure match-rules suite** (`module/src/matchRules.test.ts`, runs CLI-free in `pnpm test` like N0's): handshake matrix (both tracks × wrong-compiler/wrong-hash/wrong-protocol/wrong-balance), multi-match isolation, contested pickups, per-weapon validation tables, energy settlement incl. boost inference and fatal zero-crossing, respawn lifecycle, countdown clamp, connection-generation supersession.
2. **Adversarial fabrication suite [replaces the impossible "0 % fabricated" gate]:** scripted hostile clients attempt enumerated cheats (fly-the-chord, gate-claim-from-elsewhere, boosting-flag evasion, mine-trigger omission, out-of-envelope and in-envelope hit fabrications, unheld-weapon fire, grace-window hits, double-claim pickups); the suite asserts exactly which are rejected outright, which are bounded (and by what), and fails if any *bound* regresses. The residual list is a checked-in document — the enforcement teeth behind §5.
3. **Simulated-latency harness** (`src/net/netsim/`): deterministic event-queue over the pure rules + per-client delay/jitter/burst queues (bursts model TCP HOL, the realistic failure); scripted kinematic bot clients publish at 15 Hz, fire, claim from a 120 ms-interpolated view. Budgets at 50/100/200 ms RTT + burst profiles: zero valid-flow rejections; energy divergence ≤ ε at settle; specter-leading-by->RTT always spoofs; respawn round-trip ≤ 1 s + RTT + margin; telegraph-lead histograms published per bucket (x9's measured-metrics style).
4. **Bot-vs-bot network soaks** (`scripts/net-soak.mjs`): seeds × both tracks × RTT/burst profiles × 8 bots, weapons hot; invariants: no negative energy, double kill, stuck-destroyed, orphaned entities/schedules, cross-match leaks, or unfinished match past the ceiling. PR-bounded (10 races/track) and release (100 races/track + 10 % disconnect churn) tiers [x9's tiering].
5. **Maincloud measurement + real-transport smokes** (M1 onward, `kzero-p4-dev`/`-stage`): scripted 8-client publish storm recording p50/p95 reducer RTT, subscription-delta latency, and the multi-dimensional usage metering; version-skew and hot-swap-reconnect smokes; piloted QA per milestone under DevTools throttling (0/100/150/250 ms), documented in `docs/qa/phase-p4-*` per the existing gate conventions.
6. **Kept regression floor:** golden hash, combat replay, compiler replay, both-track line-bot, fall-through gate, `track:check` + new generated-data drift checks.

## 14. Migration (what stays / what changes)

**Untouched:** fixed-tick runtime and system order; craft controller/physics/Rapier adapter; all solo modes; pure combat/energy/items math; track compiler and artifacts; feel package; audio; persistence. **Changed:** `spacetime.ts` (env config, admission-aware connect, scoped subscriptions, clock offset, regenerated bindings — requires a CLI-equipped environment, `src/net/bindings/*` headers confirm generated provenance); `matchAdapter.ts` (match context + combat wrappers); `raceBridge.ts` (per-track gates); `transformSync.ts` (+`boosting`); new `combatNetBridge.ts` and `onlineShell.ts`/`MatchBrowser.tsx`; `PlayerCraft.tsx`'s `soloLiteralRespawn` boolean fork becomes a `MatchAuthority` strategy object (solo = today's path byte-identical; online = predicted-energy/server-settled, bridge-routed combat, token-driven respawn). Honest sizing **[x9-corrected]**: the online authority path is substantial new network code (three ownership classes, claims protocol, lazy settlement, respawn arbitration), not mere re-plumbing — reflected in M3–M6 being the plan's slowest miles.
